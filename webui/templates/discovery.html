💻 EHR & Clinical Systems

The EHR platform and its configuration are the largest drivers of log volume. These questions should be directed to the Epic (or EHR) team lead or CMIO's office.

Why: Epic generates the most detailed audit trails (SIEM/audit logs). Cerner uses a different audit model. This changes volume calculations by 2–3×.

Ask: "During your busiest shift (typically weekday mornings), how many clinicians are simultaneously logged into Epic/Cerner?" Include Hyperspace, Haiku (mobile), Canto (iPad), and Rover.

Why: Each concurrent user generates 800–5,000 SIEM events/day depending on audit level. This is used as a cross-check against bed-based volume estimates.

Why: Each active module adds ~8% to SIEM audit volume. A fully-loaded Epic instance (12+ modules) generates ~2× the audit trail of a basic 4-module deployment.

If the customer doesn't know, assume "Standard." Ask: "Has your compliance team ever requested expanded audit logging beyond Epic defaults?"

Why: This is the single largest volume swing factor. Verbose audit generates 3–8× the data of standard audit. Knowing this accurately can prevent a 5× quoting error.

🔴 Critical — 3-8× volume impact

Ask: "How many unique patients log into MyChart per month?" If unavailable, estimate as 30–40% of annual unique patients.

Why: Each active patient generates 1.5–16 log events/day (portal sessions, message sends, results views). A health system with 200K active MyChart users can generate 300K–3.2M events/day from portal alone.

Why: If they have an existing SIEM, ask for their current daily ingest volume (GiB/day). This is the single most accurate data point you can get — it tells you actual production log volume without any estimation.

⭐ Gold — Ask for current GiB/day ingest if available

This is the most valuable single data point. Ask: "What's your current daily log ingest into Splunk/Sentinel?" Even a rough number (e.g., "about 50 gigs a day") massively improves quote accuracy.

🔌 Integration & Interoperability

Integration engines and HL7/FHIR interfaces are major log generators. Direct these questions to the integration team or interface analysts.

Why: Mirth Connect exposes 6 metrics/channel at 1-min resolution. Other engines have different metric profiles. Knowing the platform determines metric DDU calculations.

Ask: "How many active channels are running in your Mirth/Rhapsody instance?" Include both inbound and outbound. Production channels only — exclude dev/test.

Why: Each Mirth channel generates 8,640 metric DDUs/day at 1-min polling. 30 channels = 259K DDUs/day. 200 channels = 1.7M DDUs/day.

Count distinct HL7 connections to ancillary systems: LIS (lab), RIS (radiology), pharmacy, blood bank, device interfaces, billing, registration, etc. Ask: "How many ancillary systems send/receive HL7 messages?"

Why: Each interface generates 800–3,000 HL7 messages/day (~1.5 KB avg). 25 interfaces ≈ 20K–75K messages/day ≈ 30–112 MB/day of log data.

Most integration teams know this number. Ask: "What's your daily HL7 message throughput?" Check Mirth dashboard → Channel Statistics.

⭐ Gold — Direct measurement beats estimation

Why: FHIR API call volume ranges from near-zero (HL7v2-only shops) to millions/day (active Care Everywhere with 100+ partners). This can be a 0–10 GiB/day swing.

Ask: "How many external organizations exchange data with you via Care Everywhere, CommonWell, or Carequality?" Larger systems may have 200+ partners.

Why: Each active partner generates 500–5,000 FHIR API calls/day. 50 partners at midpoint = 137K calls/day ≈ 65 MB/day.

Clarity/Caboodle data extracts, warehouse loads, analytics feeds, regulatory reporting exports.

Why: ETL job logging is a small but measurable contributor (20–80 log records per interface/day). Mostly relevant for completeness.

🌐 Network Infrastructure

Network devices are typically the highest-volume log sources (syslog + NetFlow). These questions go to the network engineering or infrastructure team.

Count all SNMP/syslog-managed devices: core switches, distribution switches, access switches, wireless controllers, APs (if individually managed), routers, load balancers. Ask: "How many devices are in your network monitoring system (SolarWinds, PRTG, LibreNMS)?"

Why: Each device generates 200–200K syslog events/day (depending on verbosity) and 38–540 SNMP metric DDUs/day (depending on poll interval). This is often the #1 or #2 largest data source.

Palo Alto, Fortinet, Cisco ASA/FTD, Check Point, Sophos. Include all perimeter, internal segmentation, and DMZ firewalls. Ask: "How many firewall appliances (physical + virtual) are in production?"

Why: Firewalls generate 3–10× more syslog than switches and are the primary NetFlow source. A single Palo Alto on a busy 1Gbps link can produce 10–20M flow records/day at 1:1 sampling.

🟠 High — Firewalls are often the largest single log source

Why: Palo Alto and Fortinet have the most verbose default syslog. Cisco ASA is more compact. Vendor choice affects average log line size (200–800 bytes).

If they don't know, assume "Warnings + Errors" (the most common default). Ask: "Are your switches/firewalls sending syslog to a collector today? What severity level?"

🔴 Critical — 10-50× volume difference between error-only and debug

Why: NetFlow at 1:1 can generate 40–160M records/day for an 8-firewall campus (16–64 GiB/day). At 1:100 sampling, that drops to 400K–1.6M records (160–640 MB/day). This single setting can be the difference between a 20 DPS and 80 DPS quote.

🔴 Critical — Largest potential volume swing in the entire assessment

Why: A 200-device network at 1-min polling generates ~13M DDUs/day. At 5-min polling, that drops to ~2.6M DDUs/day.

If they have a syslog collector (Splunk, Graylog, rsyslog), ask: "What's your daily syslog volume?" This replaces all network estimation.

⭐ Gold — Direct measurement beats estimation

🔒 Security & Compliance

Compliance requirements directly affect log retention and audit verbosity. These questions go to the CISO or compliance officer.

Why: SOX and HITRUST typically require "extended" audit logging. PCI DSS mandates 1-year log retention. HIPAA requires 6-year policy retention but log retention varies. These drive both volume and storage costs.

Why: Retention is a linear multiplier on storage DPS. 365 days of retention on 50 GiB/day ingest = 18,250 GiB stored = ~64 storage DPS/day. This is often the largest cost surprise.

🟠 High — Directly multiplies storage costs

Why: BTG monitoring adds detailed access logs for restricted records. Volume impact is moderate (2–5% increase) but it indicates the org has higher audit requirements overall.

Why: Insider threat programs (e.g., Protenus, Imprivata FairWarning) typically require verbose audit logs. If active/planned, audit level is likely "High" or "Extended" — validate this against Section 2.
